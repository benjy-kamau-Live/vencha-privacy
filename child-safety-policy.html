Child Safety Standards & CSAE Policy

Last Updated: November 21, 2025

ZERO TOLERANCE FOR CHILD SEXUAL ABUSE & EXPLOITATION
Our Commitment: Vencha is committed to protecting children and preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our platform. We have implemented comprehensive policies, procedures, and technologies to create a safe environment for all users.

1. Age Requirements & Verification

Minimum Age: Users must be at least 13 years old to create a Vencha account. We strictly enforce this requirement through:

  • Age verification during account registration
  • Automated detection of underage users through behavioral analysis
  • Immediate account suspension for users found to be under 13
  • Regular audits to identify and remove underage accounts
  • Parent/guardian notification when underage accounts are detected

For Users 13-17: We provide additional protections for teenage users including:

  • Enhanced privacy settings by default
  • Restricted interaction with adult users (18+)
  • Additional content moderation on their posts
  • Parental controls and oversight options

2. Content Moderation & Detection

2.1 Automated Systems

We employ advanced technology to detect and prevent CSAE content:

  • AI-Powered Image Scanning: All uploaded photos and videos are automatically scanned for CSAE indicators
  • Hash Matching: We use PhotoDNA and other hash-matching technologies to identify known CSAE material
  • Text Analysis: AI algorithms detect grooming language, solicitation attempts, and inappropriate conversations
  • Behavioral Detection: Machine learning identifies suspicious patterns (e.g., adults targeting minors)
  • Real-Time Blocking: Suspected CSAE content is blocked immediately before it's published

2.2 Human Review Team

  • Trained content moderators review flagged content 24/7
  • Specialized team for handling child safety reports
  • Escalation protocols for urgent cases
  • Regular training on CSAE identification and trauma-informed practices
  • Support and mental health resources for moderators

2.3 Prohibited Content

The following content is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate removal and account termination:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form
  • Sexually suggestive content involving minors
  • Content that sexualizes, grooms, or exploits children
  • Solicitation or facilitation of child exploitation
  • Content promoting or normalizing child sexual abuse
  • Sharing of nude or sexual imagery of minors, including self-generated content
  • Age-inappropriate sexual content accessible to minors

3. Reporting Mechanisms

3.1 In-App Reporting

We provide multiple easy-to-access reporting options within the Vencha app:

  • Report Button: Available on all profiles, challenges, comments, and messages
  • Emergency Report: Fast-track reporting for urgent child safety concerns
  • Anonymous Reporting: Users can report without revealing their identity
  • Category Selection: Specific "Child Safety" report category for priority handling

3.3 What to Report

Please report any of the following immediately:

  • Suspected child sexual abuse material
  • Adults attempting to contact or groom minors
  • Solicitation of minors for sexual purposes
  • Sharing of nude or sexual images involving minors
  • Accounts impersonating minors
  • Content promoting child exploitation
  • Any behavior that puts children at risk

4. Our Response Protocol

4.1 Immediate Actions

When CSAE content or behavior is detected or reported:

  1. Content Removal: Suspected CSAE content is removed immediately (within minutes)
  2. Account Suspension: Offending accounts are immediately suspended pending investigation
  3. Evidence Preservation: All relevant data is preserved for law enforcement
  4. User Protection: Affected users (especially minors) are notified and provided support resources

4.2 Investigation & Enforcement

  • Thorough investigation of all reports within 24 hours
  • Permanent account termination for confirmed violations
  • Device and IP bans to prevent re-registration
  • Cross-platform coordination to identify repeat offenders
  • Documentation of all findings for potential legal action

4.3 Law Enforcement Cooperation

We work closely with law enforcement agencies in Kenya and internationally to combat CSAE:

  • Mandatory Reporting: We report all CSAE incidents to:
    • Kenya Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) - Anti-Human Trafficking & Child Protection Unit
    • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) - CyberTipline (for international cases)
    • INTERPOL (for cross-border cases)
  • Evidence Sharing: We provide law enforcement with all relevant evidence including:
    • User account information and registration details
    • Content metadata (upload times, device info, location data)
    • Communication logs between users
    • IP addresses and technical identifiers
  • Rapid Response: We respond to law enforcement requests within 24 hours
  • Legal Process: We comply with all court orders, subpoenas, and search warrants

5. Prevention Measures

5.1 Platform Design

Our app is designed with child safety built-in:

  • Limited Private Messaging: Restrictions on adults messaging minors they don't know
  • Public-First: Challenges and interactions are primarily public (visible to moderators)
  • No Location Sharing: Precise location data is never shared with other users
  • Content Filters: Age-appropriate content filtering based on user age
  • Privacy by Default: Enhanced privacy settings for users under 18

5.2 User Education

  • In-app safety tips and resources for parents and teens
  • Regular safety reminders and notifications
  • Guides on recognizing grooming and exploitation
  • Information on reporting and blocking features
  • Links to external resources (e.g., helplines, counseling services)

5.3 Community Standards

Our Community Guidelines explicitly prohibit:

  • Any form of child exploitation or abuse
  • Sharing personal information of minors
  • Adult content accessible to minors
  • Harassment or targeting of minors
  • Impersonation of minors by adults

6. Compliance with Laws & Regulations

6.1 Kenyan Legislation

We comply with all relevant Kenyan laws protecting children:

  • The Children Act, 2022: Protection of children's rights and welfare
  • Sexual Offences Act, 2006: Criminalization of child sexual abuse and exploitation
  • Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, 2018: Prohibition of cyber-based child exploitation
  • Data Protection Act, 2019: Special protections for children's personal data
  • Counter-Trafficking in Persons Act, 2010: Prevention of child trafficking

6.2 International Standards

  • UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
  • Optional Protocol on the Sale of Children, Child Prostitution and Child Pornography
  • African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child
  • NCMEC guidelines for online service providers
  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) best practices

6.3 Industry Commitments

We are committed to following industry best practices:

  • Technology Coalition Principles to Combat Online Child Sexual Exploitation and Abuse
  • Alliance to Counter Crime Online (ACCO) guidelines
  • WeProtect Global Alliance model national response framework
